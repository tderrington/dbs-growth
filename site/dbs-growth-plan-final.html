Derrington Building Studio

Studio Growth Plan

A phased roadmap from the current three-person studio to a 13-person practice. Each phase is triggered by revenue — not ambition — and designed so the studio grows without anyone burning out along the way. This document is for everyone in the studio. It describes where we are, where we're headed, and what each person's role looks like at every stage.

Guiding principle. No phase is initiated on projected revenue — only on work in hand or under contract. Each hire should pay for itself before the next one is needed. Growth is a consequence of the work, not a goal unto itself.

00 Current State

3 people · Revenue: ~$600K – 900K

The studio currently operates as a three-person team with everyone carrying multiple roles. Tim serves as principal, studio director, marketing lead, technical coordinator, spec writer, detailer, and occasional senior project architect. The two staff members each carry a mix of project architecture, design, field work, interiors, and technical coordination. This works because the team is strong — but it means the firm cannot take on meaningfully more work without someone becoming a bottleneck.

The immediate constraint is that the principal's time is split across seven functions. Every hour spent on specs, detailing, or admin is an hour unavailable for business development, design leadership, and client relationships — the activities that generate new revenue and new opportunity for the entire team.

01 Phase 1 — Immediate Relief

3 FTE + 2 Colombia contracts + 1 remote hire + in-person studio support · Next 1–3 months

Three moves, executed in parallel. First, contract drafting and detailing support from Colombia — this reduces CD production pressure on the entire team immediately. Second, a remote Studio Coordinator takes over all screen-based administrative work: invoicing, timesheets, AP/AR, HR paperwork, contract administration. Third, a US-based specification consultant or SpecLink platform takes over spec writing, which requires American code fluency that international contracts cannot reliably provide.

In-person building tasks — mail, packages, vendor visits, building maintenance — continue to be handled on a flexible, informal basis by studio-adjacent support.

What changes for the team. The Colombian contracts absorb the CD production grind — drafting sheets and pulling details that currently eat into everyone's evenings. Staff A can focus on field coordination, technical resolution, and ArchiCAD standards. Staff B can focus on design, interiors, and client-facing work. Tim sheds spec writing, detailing, and administrative tasks, dropping from seven roles to four.

02 Phase 2 — The Leverage Hire

5–6 FTE + contracts · 6–12 months out · Revenue trigger: ~$1.4–1.8M

This is the most important phase in the entire plan. A Senior Project Architect joins the studio — someone who can own a project end-to-end, from schematic design through construction administration, without the principal managing day-to-day delivery. This single hire changes the studio's ceiling. It creates a delivery structure that runs while the principal is meeting a client, visiting a site, or developing the next project.

A Project Designer joins alongside the Senior PA, providing dedicated design and visualization capacity for a full project team. Meanwhile, the existing team begins to specialize along the lines that are already natural.

Why this hire changes everything. A Senior PA who can own delivery end-to-end creates a project team that runs without the principal in the room. That frees the principal to pursue work the studio couldn't take before — which is the only way revenue grows beyond the current ceiling. Every subsequent hire in this plan depends on this one working.

The existing team's evolution becomes explicit in this phase. Staff A's natural strengths in construction, field coordination, and ArchiCAD management position them on a trajectory toward the studio's Technical Coordinator & BIM Lead and eventually the Specifications & Field Lead role. Staff B's design and interiors strengths position them as the future lead of the studio's interiors and experience design capability — with the eventual authority to manage a dedicated interiors team member.

03 Phase 3 — Two Project Teams

9–10 FTE + contracts · 12–24 months out · Revenue trigger: ~$2.0–2.6M

The studio now has enough sustained work to support two distinct project teams. A second Senior PA joins to lead Team B. The original team members have fully specialized: Staff A operates as the studio's Technical Coordinator, BIM Lead, and growing Specifications & Field authority. Staff B leads design and interiors, now with a dedicated Experience & Interiors Designer reporting to them — the team member they've been building toward leading.

Additional hires this phase include a second Project Designer, a dedicated Architectural Designer (FTE), and potentially a remote Marketing & BD Coordinator to take that responsibility entirely off the principal's plate. The Studio Coordinator role may expand to a full Studio Manager if operational complexity warrants it.

04 Phase 4 — Full Studio

13 FTE + contracts · 24+ months · Revenue trigger: $2.9–3.3M

The complete structure. Two full project teams, each led by a Senior PA, with a Project Architect as swing capacity and bench depth. A dedicated Technical Coordinator & BIM Lead — the role Staff A has grown into — owns QA/QC and ArchiCAD standards across both teams. The Experience & Interiors Designer and Specifications & Field Lead are fully dedicated specialist roles. Operations is covered by a Studio Manager and a remote Marketing & BD Coordinator. Colombian contract drafters continue as surge capacity. In-person building support continues on a flexible basis.

The plan in one sentence. Every phase removes roles from people who are overloaded and gives them to people whose full-time job it is to do that work well. The principal goes from seven roles to two. The original team members evolve from generalists into leaders of the capabilities they're already best at. Nobody is asked to do less — everyone is asked to go deeper.

Growth plan assumes revenue triggers are met before each phase of hiring. Compensation ranges reflect Austin, TX market rates as of early 2026.
